# MARKET_FIT.md

**Step 2 — Design** | Contributed by: Product owner, with Claude facilitation

> **What is this?** Market fit is about answering one question honestly: "Would real people actually use this, and why?" This document helps you get specific about who your users are, what pain they have, and why your product is the right solution — before you spend time building something nobody asked for.

This document validates who your product is for, what problem it solves for them, and why they'd choose it over alternatives. It sharpens your thinking before you invest time building features no one needs.

---

> **Claude Guidance:** Help the user get specific. "Anyone who wants to save time" is not a target user — ask follow-up questions until there's a clear, narrow persona. Help them articulate the specific pain point their product addresses and why existing solutions fall short. Challenge assumptions gently: "You mentioned your users are busy professionals — what specifically makes current tools not work for them?" This document should inform what goes into the MVP.

---

## Target Users

*Who is this product for? Describe the primary user persona in detail: their background, their goals, the specific problem they face, and what their life looks like without this product.*

### Primary Persona

*Name (fictional is fine), role, context, and the core pain point.*

### Secondary Persona (if applicable)

*A distinct user type who also benefits, but is not the primary focus.*

## The Problem

*What problem does this product solve? Describe it from the user's perspective, not the builder's.*

## Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

*What are users doing today to address this problem? Why is that inadequate — too expensive, too complex, missing a key feature, wrong form factor?*

## Why This Product

*What does your product offer that others don't? This doesn't have to be a revolutionary differentiator — it can be simpler, cheaper, more focused, or built for a specific community.*

## Success Metrics

*How will you know if people find value in this product? What behaviors indicate it's working?*
